# Shive-Hattery — Recommended Changes
*Based on audit scored 4.4/10 — May 13, 2026*

---

## Priority 1 — Fix Geographic Context in News & Insights

**The problem:** Project announcements and news items reference cities and counties with no state identifier. For a firm operating across Illinois, Iowa, Wisconsin, and Indiana, "Linn County" or "Moline" is ambiguous to anyone outside the immediate region — and invisible to search engines targeting out-of-market clients.

**Why it matters:**
- Out-of-market prospects (the clients most worth winning) have no geographic frame of reference
- Search engines can't associate content with state-level queries like "architecture firm Iowa" or "AEC firm Chicago suburbs"
- It signals a publication habit built for local insiders, not new business

**The fix — editorial standard, no redesign required:**
- Every project mention includes City, State on first reference: *Cedar Rapids, Iowa* not *Cedar Rapids*
- Every Insights post includes a dateline or location tag: `Cedar Rapids, IA · May 2026`
- Portfolio project pages add a location line in the header: **Location:** Iowa City, Iowa
- News archive gets a filterable tag by state — Iowa, Illinois, Wisconsin, Indiana

**Suggested dateline format for Insights posts:**
```
Cedar Rapids, Iowa — [Headline]
```

---

## Priority 2 — Convert Portfolio from Gallery to Case Studies

The portfolio has 27 project pages that function as photo galleries with a caption. No challenge stated. No decision explained. No outcome measured.

**Fix:** Pick the 5 strongest projects — one per sector — and rebuild to this structure:

> **The Challenge** — what the client was trying to solve
> **Our Approach** — what made Shive-Hattery's thinking distinct
> **The Outcome** — measurable result or client quote
> **Location:** City, State · Sector · Year completed

400–600 words. No redesign. Editorial lift only.

---

## Priority 3 — Tell the 130-Year Independence Story

No AEC firm in their competitive tier has been independent for 130 years. Most have been acquired. This is a differentiation story that writes itself — and they're not telling it.

**Where it belongs:**
- Homepage: replace "Purposeful Work Done by Passionate People" with something that earns the claim
- About page: a dedicated timeline section — not a paragraph, a visual
- New business conversations: a one-page "Why Independent Matters" leave-behind

**Draft tagline direction:**
> "130 years. Still independent. Still here."
> "Built to last. Not to sell."

---

## Priority 4 — Add One Reciprocity Asset Per Sector

Zero gated content. Zero free resources. The site asks for a call before it gives anything.

**Minimum viable reciprocity — one asset to start:**
- *Pre-Design Checklist for Healthcare Facilities* (or Education, or Workplace)
- Gate with email. Delivers value. Builds list. Signals expertise.

This single asset would do more conversion work than any homepage headline rewrite.

---

## Priority 5 — Put Named People on the Homepage

"Passionate People" with no faces or names is a brand liability, not a brand asset.

**Fix:** One rotating homepage feature — a named principal or project lead with a one-sentence point of view. Rotates monthly. Costs nothing.

Example format:
> **Sarah Chen, AIA — Healthcare Studio Lead**
> "The best patient outcomes start before the first wall goes up."

---

## Priority 6 — Rewrite the Boilerplate

The worst line on the site:
> *"We take pride in seamlessly integrating our expertise with customized solutions to deliver extraordinary outcomes for our valued clients."*

Delete it. Replace with nothing. The space will be better.

Every page should be held to the standard of the best line currently on the site:
> *"Meets clients where they are — then takes them further."*

## Quick Wins (Under 1 Hour Each)

| Fix | Where | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Add state to all city references in Insights | Insights archive | SEO + out-of-market clarity |
