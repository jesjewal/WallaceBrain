## 4. Key Market Trends

- **Articulate Storyline/360 is non-negotiable:** Appears in virtually all high-paying listings. This is the #1 skill differentiator.
- **Iowa market dominated by financial services:** GreenState Credit Union, Transamerica, and UFG Insurance are the primary drivers of local ID employment in the Iowa City/Cedar Rapids corridor.
- **Iowa salary ceiling is ~$92K** for local roles, while remote fintech/software roles frequently cross $100K.
- **ADDIE remains foundational** but Agile ID and SAM (Successive Approximation Model) are increasingly required, especially at financial institutions and tech companies.
- **Video production skills are now a differentiator:** Transamerica explicitly requests Adobe After Effects and Premiere Pro; video integration with ID roles is accelerating.
- **LMS specialization matters:** Workday Learning is cited by multiple employers (Tinuiti, State of Iowa); Canvas and SCORM compliance remain standard.
- **Higher ed Iowa hiring is stagnant:** No open positions at U of Iowa, Kirkwood, or ISU as of this search cycle. Corporate sector is significantly more active.
- **Title inflation is real:** "Learning Experience Designer" and "Instructional Developer" often describe the same work as "Instructional Designer" but at companies that prefer trendier job titles (tech, SaaS, fintech). These roles frequently pay 10–15% more.
- **AI video tools are emerging as a differentiator:** Allstate explicitly requires Synthesia, HeyGen, Descript, or Vyond. Candidates who can use AI-assisted video narration are ahead of the curve.
- **Salary localization is a risk for remote roles:** DoorDash's Iowa-localized pay drops to ~$64K from a $83K national average. Always confirm whether remote pay is national or location-adjusted before applying.
