## Core Principle
Jesse is genuinely skilled across all of these domains — video production, graphic design, InDesign, writing/editing, brand strategy, AI tools, team leadership, instructional design. This is not spin or gap-covering. The tailoring is about choosing which genuinely mastered skillset to put in front of a specific audience. Every version of the resume is true. The framing is strategic, not compensatory.

The title, profile summary, and skills section order are the primary levers. Work experience stays mostly stable; the bullet language shifts to surface the relevant work that was always there.

## Pattern Notes

### Title is a signal
The resume title sets the frame for everything that follows. "Creative Strategist + Content Producer" reads as comms/content. "Art Director" reads as brand/visual craft leadership. Switch it early and let the rest follow.

### Skills block order matters
Reorder skills sections to put the most relevant cluster first. For AEC/content roles → Creative & Production leads. For agency/brand roles → same, but emphasize logo/brand identity explicitly within it.

### Cover letter bespoke work sample strategy (Shive-Hattery model)
For roles where the work itself is the credential, building something new FROM SCRATCH specifically for the application is a high-impact differentiator. Jesse did this for Shive-Hattery and it was central to the cover letter. Consider whether to replicate for Informatics.

### Cover letter openers vary by role type
- **Creative/portfolio roles** (SH): Lead with what you built — the work speaks first
- **Performance marketing roles** (TrueNorth): Lead with a personal/cultural hook that connects to the company's world, then pivot to credentials
- Never use the same opener twice — each letter should feel written for that specific company

### Gap acknowledgment as a strategy
For roles where there's a real gap (e.g., TrueNorth — no owned paid media budget), Jesse chose to name it directly rather than avoid it. Paired with "here's what I have done and how fast I close gaps." This is a deliberate choice — use when the gap is likely to be noticed anyway and honesty + confidence is a better read than silence.

### What to always include regardless of role
- 20+ years framing (establishes seniority)
- AI practitioner credential (not casual — workflows, systems thinking)
- Mentorship / team development (present in every role)
- Quick learner / growth mindset language (genuine and proven)

### The "hidden career" reframe (UFG model)
The same 20 years of work can read as multimedia production, marketing strategy, creative direction, OR instructional design — depending entirely on which bullet language you choose. For IDT roles, rewrite every job's bullets to surface the teaching, training, curriculum, and learning design work that was always there but described in other terms. The facts don't change; the frame does.

### What to leave in the background for creative/design roles
- Instructional design & LMS platforms
- Economic/workforce data analysis
- Grant writing
- Community & workforce development
